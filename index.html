I am an operator and analyst whose career has been defined less by industry than by instinct — the ability to enter an unfamiliar domain, find the underlying logic, and build something executable around it.

My background spans the marine industry, aviation infrastructure, enterprise healthcare technology, and capital investment. What connects these isn't a straight line — it's a thread. Each environment taught me something about how complex systems fail and how the right kind of attention can prevent it.

I hold an FAA Instrument Rating, which I think of less as a credential and more as evidence of a particular way of engaging with complexity: learn the rules, trust the instruments, and make decisions under pressure with incomplete information.

I'm based in Saint Augustine, Florida, actively exploring where pattern recognition and operational fluency can create the most leverage — in aviation, in supply chain, or wherever the next interesting problem turns out to be.
